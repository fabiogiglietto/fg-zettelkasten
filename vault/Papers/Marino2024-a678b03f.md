# THE BRAZILIAN DIGITAL BATTLEFIELD: INVESTIGATING THE DYNAMICS OF POLITICAL INFORMATION CAMPAIGNS IN POST-BOLSONARO ERA

## Summary

This paper investigates the dynamics of political information campaigns in Brazil following Jair Bolsonaro's presidency, framing the country's digital sphere as a contested "battlefield" where political actors and their supporters mobilize information across platforms. The work situates itself within emerging scholarship on digital political communication, influence operations, and information disorder in the Global South, examining how the political fragmentation and polarization characteristic of the Bolsonaro years continue to shape online contestation in its aftermath. Because no abstract or full text was available, the specific argumentative thrust, evidentiary base, and conclusions of the study cannot be reconstructed in detail.

## Key Contributions

- Brings empirical attention to political information campaigns in Brazil during a transitional political period that has received less systematic study than the Bolsonaro presidency itself.
- Extends the literature on coordinated political communication and influence operations beyond its dominant Anglophone and European cases.
- Frames the Brazilian post-Bolsonaro digital environment as a continuing site of organized political contestation rather than a return to normalcy.
