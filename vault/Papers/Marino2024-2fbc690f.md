# Integrating Large Language Models in Political Discourse Studies on Social Media: Challenges of Validating an LLMs-in-the-loop Pipeline

## Summary

Marino and Giglietto address the methodological challenges of embedding Large Language Models within multi-step research pipelines for analyzing political discourse on social media. They frame this approach as "LLMs-in-the-loop" — where generative models serve as components in a broader analytical workflow rather than as standalone tools — and argue that this configuration raises distinctive validation problems that go beyond conventional NLP evaluation practices. The paper contributes a reflective, methodologically-oriented intervention into how computational social scientists can responsibly integrate LLMs into empirical studies of political communication.

## Key Contributions

- Articulates and names the "LLMs-in-the-loop" pipeline as a distinct methodological configuration in political communication research.
- Identifies validation challenges specific to using LLMs as embedded components in computational social science workflows.
- Offers methodological guidance for researchers seeking to ensure reliability and validity when LLMs handle classification or annotation tasks on social media discourse.

## Methods

The paper takes a methodological-discussion approach, reflecting on the integration of LLMs into social media political discourse pipelines and focusing on validation procedures for LLM-assisted classification and annotation. Specific empirical demonstrations are not detailed in the available metadata.

## Findings

- LLMs can be productively integrated into political discourse analysis pipelines, but their use introduces distinctive validation requirements.
- Standard NLP validation practices are insufficient for the "LLMs-in-the-loop" setting; dedicated methodological scrutiny is needed when LLMs operate as workflow components.
