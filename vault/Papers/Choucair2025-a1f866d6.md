# TRANSFORMATIVE TOOLS, EMERGING CHALLENGES: EMPIRICAL AND PRACTICAL EXPERIENCES WITH LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS FOR TEXT CLASSIFICATION AND ANNOTATION IN COMMUNICATION STUDIES

## Summary

This paper offers a collective reflection from multiple research teams in communication studies on their empirical and practical experiences using large language models (LLMs) for text classification and annotation. It frames LLMs as transformative tools that reshape established workflows in automated content analysis, while simultaneously surfacing a set of emerging methodological and practical challenges that the field has yet to fully resolve. The contribution is positioned less as a single empirical study and more as a synthesis of multi-team experience aimed at guiding responsible adoption.

## Key Contributions

- Aggregates practical, hands-on experience with LLM-based classification and annotation across several communication research groups.
- Articulates the dual character of LLMs in this domain: transformative capability paired with new methodological risks.
- Surfaces challenges relevant to researchers integrating generative AI into established annotation and content-analytic workflows.

## Methods

The paper synthesizes empirical and practical experiences from multiple collaborating research teams that have applied LLMs to text classification and annotation tasks in communication studies. Specific technical procedures, models, or validation strategies are not detailed in the available material.

## Findings

- LLMs are presented as genuinely transformative for text classification and annotation in communication studies.
- Their adoption raises emerging methodological and practical challenges that require disciplinary attention.
