# TRANSFORMATIVE TOOLS, EMERGING CHALLENGES: EMPIRICAL AND PRACTICAL EXPERIENCES WITH LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS FOR TEXT CLASSIFICATION AND ANNOTATION IN COMMUNICATION STUDIES

## Summary

This collectively authored paper reflects on the rapid uptake of large language models (LLMs) as tools for text classification and annotation in communication studies. Drawing together empirical and practical experiences from multiple research teams, it positions LLMs as transformative for automated content analysis while flagging emerging methodological challenges that the field must address. The piece reads as a stocktaking intervention: a synthesis aimed at consolidating what researchers have learned about deploying LLMs in place of, or alongside, traditional manual and supervised coding pipelines.

## Key Contributions

- A collective, multi-team assessment of LLM-based text classification and annotation in communication research.
- Articulation of both the transformative affordances and the methodological/practical risks these tools introduce.
- Practical lessons relevant to researchers adopting LLMs for content analysis workflows.
