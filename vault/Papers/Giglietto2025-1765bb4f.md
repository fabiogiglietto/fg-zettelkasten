# "A Pretty Blunt Approach": Meta's Political Content Reduction Policy and Italian Parliamentarians' Facebook Visibility

## Summary

This paper by Fabio Giglietto investigates how Meta's policy of reducing the distribution of political content on Facebook has affected the visibility of Italian parliamentarians on the platform. Framing Meta's intervention as "a pretty blunt approach," the study interrogates whether algorithmic deprioritization of political content treats all political actors and content types uniformly, and what this means for elected representatives' ability to reach constituents. The work situates platform governance decisions as consequential for democratic communication, using the Italian parliament as an empirical case.

## Key Contributions

- An empirical case study of how a platform-level content policy reshapes the visibility of elected representatives in a national political system.
- Evidence relevant to ongoing debates about Meta's political content reduction policy and its democratic implications.
- A characterization of the policy as a "blunt instrument" that does not finely distinguish among political actors or types of political content.
