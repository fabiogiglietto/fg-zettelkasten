# "A Pretty Blunt Approach": Meta's Political Content Reduction Policy and Italian Parliamentarians' Facebook Visibility

## Summary

This paper examines the downstream effects of Meta's policy to reduce the visibility of political content on Facebook, using Italian parliamentarians as a case study. Giglietto frames the policy — captured in the titular "pretty blunt approach" — as a platform governance decision with measurable consequences for how elected officials reach constituents through algorithmic curation. By focusing on Italy rather than the United States, the paper extends ongoing debates about platform accountability and algorithmic deprioritization into a European political-communication context.

## Key Contributions

- Provides empirical evidence on how Meta's political content reduction policy affects the Facebook visibility of elected officials.
- Adds an Italian and broader European case to a platform-governance literature heavily skewed toward U.S. contexts.
- Contributes to debates about algorithmic curation, democratic visibility, and platform accountability for political communication.

## Methods

A case study of Italian parliamentarians' Facebook accounts, examining engagement and reach metrics in relation to Meta's announced reduction of political content in algorithmic recommendations. Specific methodological details are not available from the provided material.
