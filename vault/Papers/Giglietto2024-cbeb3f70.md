# Evaluating Embedding Models for Clustering Italian Political News: A Comparative Study of Text-Embedding-3-Large and UmBERTo

## Summary

This paper offers a methodological comparison of two text embedding models — OpenAI's proprietary multilingual Text-Embedding-3-Large and the Italian-specific UmBERTo — applied to the task of clustering Italian political news articles. The central question is practical: which family of embeddings better captures the semantic structure of Italian political discourse for downstream unsupervised analysis? The work contributes to a broader debate over whether large general-purpose multilingual models now obviate the need for dedicated language-specific encoders in non-English NLP pipelines.

## Key Contributions

- A direct head-to-head benchmark of a frontier multilingual embedding model against a language-specific Italian model on a politically relevant clustering task.
- Practical guidance for researchers in Italian political communication and computational social science choosing between proprietary and open, language-specific tooling.
- A contribution to the wider methodological literature on embedding model selection for non-English text analysis.

## Methods

The study uses a comparative evaluation design, generating document embeddings for Italian political news articles with both Text-Embedding-3-Large and UmBERTo and then assessing clustering output produced from each representation.
